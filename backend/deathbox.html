The Financial Afterlife Kit ‚Äî so your family never has to guess what you left behind

The Problem

When someone dies, their family has NO CLUE what financial accounts, benefits, insurance, debts, or subscriptions exist.

The average American has 12+ financial accounts. A massive chunk of their financial life is locked inside their employer's payroll system (often ADP). Families spend 6-12 months just FINDING everything. Meanwhile, COBRA deadlines pass (60 days), life insurance goes unclaimed (billions sit unclaimed), PTO payouts are forgotten, and Netflix keeps charging a dead person's card.

DeathBox lets you record your financial life through simple voice conversations while you're alive, and automatically delivers an organized "Afterlife Package" to your family when you're gone.

Tech Explainer

Solana ‚Äî explained like you're 5

ü§î What is a blockchain?

Imagine a notebook that sits in the middle of a city square. Anyone can READ it. When you write something in it, it's permanent ‚Äî no eraser works on it. And every page has a timestamp and a signature proving WHO wrote it and WHEN. Nobody owns the notebook. It just... exists. That's a blockchain.

ü§î What is Solana specifically?

Solana is ONE specific blockchain (like how Chrome is one specific browser). It's popular because it's fast (processes things in under a second) and cheap (writing something costs less than $0.01). Other blockchains like Ethereum are slower and more expensive. For a hackathon, Solana is perfect.

ü§î What is a "hash"?

A hash is like a fingerprint for data. You take ANY amount of data (an entire document, a single word, whatever) and run it through a math formula. Out comes a fixed-length string like: a7f3b9c2e8d1...

The magic: the same data ALWAYS produces the same hash. But change even ONE character and the hash is completely different. So if I store a hash on the blockchain, anyone can later verify: "Is this document the same one that was hashed on that date?" If the hash matches ‚Üí untampered. If not ‚Üí someone changed it.

ü§î What is a "dead man's switch"?

A dead man's switch is a mechanism that activates when you STOP doing something. In old trains, the driver had to hold a lever. If they let go (fell asleep, had a heart attack), the train would stop automatically.

For DeathBox: you check in periodically (open the app, press a button). If you don't check in for, say, 30 days, the system assumes something happened to you and releases your Afterlife Package to your designated person.

In our hackathon demo: We won't build a fully automated Solana smart contract for this (too complex for 24hrs). Instead, our backend checks the last check-in timestamp, and if it's past the threshold, it unlocks access. The Solana part just stores the hash as proof. We explain the full vision in the pitch.

ü§î What is a "wallet" (Phantom)?

A Solana wallet is like a bank account for blockchain stuff. Phantom is a popular wallet app (browser extension). To write anything on Solana, you need a wallet with a tiny amount of SOL (Solana's currency) to pay the transaction fee (~$0.001).

For the hackathon: We use Solana's "devnet" ‚Äî a free test version of Solana where you get fake SOL for free. Everything works identically to real Solana, but costs nothing. This is standard practice for hackathon demos.
